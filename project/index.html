About Howard Wing

Childhood

I must have been about four years of age that I can vague remember that I live with my parents and sister Vera in Holland across the river called “Over de Ei” in Amsterdam.

I remember that my sister had a large toy dog that she could sit on and it had wheels under the paws and a trigger on part of its neck that as it was pulled it barked.

I had all kinds of toys, and a Nr. 1 Mecano set. The parts were spread all over the house including the balcony. In fact I was too small to have such a precious toy. As I remember, I made a type of bow and arrow and started shooting the parts away over the balcony. When I became older I started being more cautious, carefully storing the parts away after playing with it. (Later dates I kept getting additional follow-up boxes up to Nr. 5 Box.)

After moving to England, I can remember living near the tower of London as I climbed often up on a wall separating the pavement from the tower area. It was about four feet high and about three feet wide—as far as I can remember. Once I fell off the wall on my back. I could hardly regain my breath. I learned a lesson and never climbed up that wall ever since.

The Mindress 99 Algate London was quite a busy street where we lived on. My parents were told that I almost got run over by one of those large double-deck-busses that are typical in London. I was not aware that it happened to me.

I was allowed to join the cub-scouts in London when I was about seven and went to the St. John Cas School. I won a drawing-contest and received a certificate with a box with color paint. I remember I drew a tiger.

I had a friend Jeffery Street. His father was apparently a kind of superintendent of a large office building that had a flat roof and became our playground from time to time.

I was given a small dog and one day it got loose and ran away over the tower bridge. I ran partially after it, but I gave up. He was too fast, so I lost my doggy and never saw him anymore.

After living in London for more than three years, we all went back to Rotterdam, Holland. At school I was put back 2 years in my class, as I could not speak Dutch. (In the same classroom where Cathy was, who at a later date became my wife in 1945.)

After we passed our 6 Primary years in school, Cathy went to the ULO (Uitgebreid Lager Onderwijs) for three years. And I went to the MULO (Meerder Uitgebreid Lager Onderwijs) for four years.
